Discovr

The shift of music consumption to the digital sphere has created segmented and divisive patterns in how users discover music. Discovr is a music discovery app that allows users discover new music holistically.

CHALLENGE

With the rise of festivals and digital music blogs, the desire of users to consume music experientially (through concerts) and thoroughly by doing deep dives into the music users listen to has increased significantly. That being said, the prevalence of music streaming services has isolated the way in which users experience music.

SOLUTION

Discovr disrupts this music streaming service space by adding an Events tracker that allows users to see new music events near them, and an About Me page to allow users to do deep dives into the artists and genres that interests them.

user surveys

UNDERSTANDING THE NEEDS OF THE USER

After conducting user surveys, I was able to identify a few needs:
20% of users communicated that they need to identify files easily in a cloud.
48% of users reported wanting a more organized storage space for their files.
36% of users added “real-time collaboration” on their wishlist.
Alternatively, I was also able to determine a few painpoints. The major standouts were concerns about privacy and inadequate storage space.

competitive analysis

SEEING WHAT ELSE IS OUT THERE

I conducted a competitive analysis between four different companies: Spotify, Apple Music, Resident Advisor, and Shazam. I then created a visual that focused solely on the strengths and weaknesses in features alone to help understand how these brands were answering these big problems.

user personas

CAPTURING THE NATURE AND MOTIVATIONS OF THE USER

I created personas to target two main categories of users for Discovr.

user stories

PRIORITIZING BEHAVIORS

I then took on the task of creating user stories to dictate a clear priority of what users need in order to use a minimum viable product. This assessed what users would absolutely need in order to use a music discovery app, what they would like to have, and nice-to-have's.

user flows

IRONING OUT THE DETAILS

I created a content strategy, site map, and drew out user flows to truly understand what the visual interface would look like. I started with a pen and paper, and then moved onto digital versions to assess if these flows were efficien and user-friendly, or confusing.

wireframes

LAYING THE FOUNDATION

After sketching these wireframes out with pen and paper, and I made digital wireframes with Adobe XD to get a clickable prototype for usability testing. I completed several versions of these digital wireframes to iterate more quickly in the future when moving to mockups.

high fidelity mockups

TAKING IN ALL THE DETAILS

After a successful few rounds of usability testing for my wireframes and a completed brand strategy, I moved into creating polished mockups for the app. I moved through several iterations of these mockups as well before finalizing and prototyping the final deliverable with Figma's prototyping tool.

usability testing

USER FEEDBACK AND TAKEAWAYS

The final round of usability tests were invaluable. I walked out of these tests with critical feedback and updated the design to decrease confusion with some of the flows.
